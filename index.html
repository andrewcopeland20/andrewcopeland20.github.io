Random Positioning Machine (RPM)

Machine capable of reproducing microgravity conditions (less than 0.01g in 20 min operation) via two nested, independently rotating frames.

CAD Prototyping Testing

Overview: Two-axis random positioning machine for simulated microgravity research.

  • Problem: Enable long-duration microgravity exposure for biological samples without access to spaceflight or parabolic flights.
  • Approach: Designed a full mechanical system in SolidWorks; implemented a randomized trajectory generator and vector-averaging acceleration analysis in MATLAB; upgraded to a BLDC motor powertrain; fabricated mounts and couplers via laser cutting, press brake, lathe, and mill; led a 5-person team.
  • Result: Achieved vector-averaged residual acceleration < 0.01 g over 20-minute trials; increased usable working volume by ~15× in the second-generation design; delivered a modular fixture used in plant-virus experiments.

Large Diameter Centrifuge (LDC)

Rebuilt a 9-foot diameter centrifuge to accurately reproduce launch and hypergravity acceleration profiles with higher fidelity and expanded operating envelope.

Firmware Sensors MATLAB

Overview: Centrifuge

  • Problem: Reproduce realistic launch and hypergravity acceleration profiles for biological and mechanical experiments, improving agreement between commanded and measured acceleration while increasing payload capacity and maximum achievable g relative to the prior iteration.
  • Approach: Machined and rebuilt critical mechanical components for new arm and powertrain assemblies, including the main arm, swing-bucket couplers, central shaft hub, and motor retaining block; evaluated torque and jerk limits and quantified arm deflection using SolidWorks FEA.
  • Result: Achieved significantly improved agreement between commanded and measured acceleration, reducing prior braking-related underestimation by a mean of 0.22 g; enabled profiles up to ~15 g with validated structural margin (reported factor of safety ~17.7 under worst-case assumptions).
2025

Autonomous Drifter Buoy

Designed and built a compact, autonomous buoy system for characterizing the near-shore surface wave direction and spectral content off of Scripps pier.

CFD Heat Transfer Python

Overview: Autonomous Buoy

  • Problem: Estimate surface wave direction and spectral composition from a compact buoy package suitable for near-shore deployment off Scripps Pier.
  • Approach: Integrated a 9-axis IMU and GPS with an Arduino Mega; designed and fabricated a self-powered buoy housing and sensor mounts; performed IMU calibration and Earth-frame correction; implemented autonomous data logging, FFT-based spectral analysis, and motion processing routines.
  • Result: Generated directional wave spectra consistent with reference conditions after detrending and frame correction; delivered GPS trajectory, spectral, and CDIP buoy comparison plots validating system performance within expected error margins.
